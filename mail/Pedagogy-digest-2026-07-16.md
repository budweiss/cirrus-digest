## Technique spotlight: Repeated reading for fluency (with partner reading)

**What it is**

Repeated reading with partner reading involves students practicing the same text multiple times to improve their fluency and comprehension, often paired with a peer who reads along or assists as needed.

**The evidence**

Researchers like Samuels (1979) have found that repeated readings significantly enhance word recognition speed and prosody in young readers. Partner reading further supports this by providing immediate feedback and encouragement.

**Try it this week**

- Pair students into groups of two, ensuring a mix of reading abilities.
- Provide copies of an age-appropriate text for each pair to read together.
- Have pairs take turns reading aloud while their partner follows along silently or reads at the same time.
- Encourage partners to discuss any challenging words or phrases and work together to understand them.

## Podcast recaps

### Adolescent Literacy, Episode 4: A proactive response to the adolescent literacy crisis, with Kymyona Burk, Ed.D.
*Science of Reading: The Podcast*

Here's a summary of the podcast episode that is relevant and practical for teaching 3rd-grade reading/writing/English students:

Dr. Kymyona Burk, Senior Policy Fellow at ExcelinEd, joins Susan to discuss best practices in middle and high school literacy. They emphasize the importance of taking a proactive approach to adolescent literacy, similar to early literacy efforts.

Key takeaways:

1. **Universal screening**: Identify students who may be at risk for reading failure with universal screeners, providing targeted support before state assessments.
2. **Materials matter**: Ensure accurate materials and individualized reading plans for all students.
3. **Parental engagement**: Notify parents about their child's progress and involve them in middle school literacy efforts to promote a supportive environment.

Dr. Burke highlights the importance of:

* Flexible scheduling for interventions
* Providing evidence-based professional development for teachers
* Addressing advanced decoding, morphology, and vocabulary skills

Additionally, she mentions that states like Virginia and Mississippi are taking proactive steps towards adolescent literacy policy, including adopting laws that require evidence-based interventions and parental engagement.

For teaching 3rd-grade students, consider:

1. Introducing universal screeners in your classroom to identify potential reading difficulties.
2. Fostering a supportive environment by involving parents and promoting parental engagement.
3. Providing targeted support for struggling readers through evidence-based interventions.

By taking these steps, you can help prepare your students for middle school literacy challenges and set them up for success in the future.
